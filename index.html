My City

Agra is a city in northern India’s Uttar Pradesh state. It's home to the iconic Taj Mahal, a mausoleum for the Mughal ruler Shah Jahan’s wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Nearby is the Agra Fort, a fortified palace dating to the 16th and 17th centuries. Agra is also a major stop on the famed Mughal Heritage Trail.


The city was first mentioned in the epic Mahabharata, and later in the works of the Chinese traveler Xuanzang. It achieved prominence under the Mughals, who made it their capital in the 16th century.
Today, Agra is a bustling city of over 1.6 million people, and its economy is driven by tourism, agriculture, and handicrafts. The city is home to some of India’s most famous landmarks, including the Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, and Fatehpur Sikri.
